Birth of a Universe — ECSK Bounce

A massive star collapses past its event horizon. Its interior becomes a closed universe whose scale factor a̅ shrinks toward zero.

In Einstein-Cartan theory, fermion spin produces torsion — a repulsive term (−β/a̅⁴) that grows faster than gravity (1/a̅²), halting the collapse at a finite minimum scale factor a̅min.

The surface shows S² (the 2-sphere of spatial directions) evolving in proper time τ̅. Its radius at each height is the scale factor a̅(τ̅), computed from the Friedmann equation:

  (da̅/dτ̅)² = 1/a̅² − β/a̅⁴ − 1

● Bounce hypersurface — torsion halts collapse at a̅min
● Expansion — baby universe is born

The simulation is an infinitely thin 2D sensor at the red circle. It records which fluid elements on S² reach their local bounce, and when. No depth — only the hypersurface itself.
